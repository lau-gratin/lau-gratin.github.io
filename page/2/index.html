LIST 1 BIOLOGY

1、Metabolism n. me·tab·o·lism | \ mə-ˈta-bə-ˌli-zəm

​ Metabolic adj. -metabolism n.

a:the sum of the processes in the buildup and destruction of protoplasm（原生质）

specifically : the chemical changes in living cells by which energy is provided for vital processes and activities and new material is assimilated

b: the sum of the processes by which a particular substance is handled in the living body

c: the sum of the metabolic activities taking place in a particular environment the metabolism of a lake

2、Posterity n. pos·ter·i·ty | \ pä-ˈster-ə-tē

Postero- ：at the back part of

​ Synonyms:offspring fermentation

a: the offspring of one progenitor to the furthest generation

b: all future generations

3、fermentation n. fer·men·ta·tion | \ ˌfər-mən-ˈtā-shən

​ ferment v. -fermentation n.

a: a chemical change with effervescence

b: an enzymatically controlled anaerobic breakdown of an energy-rich compound (such as a carbohydrate to carbon dioxide and alcohol or to an organic acid)

4、proliferate v. pro·lif·er·ate | \ prə-ˈli-fə-ˌrā

​ proliferated; proliferating

​ proliferate v. - Proliferation n. - proliferative a.

​ Synonyms: propogate; accelerate; increse; multiply; mount; mushroom; boom…

a: to grow by rapid production of new parts, cells, buds, or offspring

b: to increase in number as if by proliferating : MULTIPLY

5、Membrane n. mem·brane | \ ˈmem-ˌbrān

1: a thin soft pliable sheet or layer especially of animal or plant origin

2: a piece of parchment forming part of a roll

6、 stodgy adj. [ˈstɑːdʒi]

Stodgy , stodgier , stodgiest

1: having a rich filling quality : heavy stodgy bread

2: moving in a slow plodding way especially as a result of physical bulkiness

3: BORING, DULLout on a peaceful rather stodgy Sunday boat trip— Edna Ferber

4: extremely old-fashioned : HIDEBOUNDreceived a pompously Victorian letter from his stodgy father— E. E. S. Montagu

7、subsist sub·sist | \ səb-ˈsist

subsisted; subsisting; subsists

a: to have existence :be

b: persist, continue

c: to have or acquire the necessities of life (such as food and clothing)

especially : to nourish oneself subsisting on roots, berries and grubs
